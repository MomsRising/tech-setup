Before you begin

Your work laptop is shipping directly from Apple. When the box arrives, you don’t need to install anything by hand. The Mac already knows it belongs to MomsRising and sets itself up for you.

Separately, you’ll get an email from us with your new MomsRising login details. Keep that email handy. You’ll need it in Step 2.

Work through the steps below in order. Tap the circle next to each item as you finish it, and your progress is saved automatically on this device.

1

Turn on your new Mac

It sets itself up automatically. Just follow the prompts.

Hands-on: a few minutes, then it works on its own
  1. Unbox the Mac, plug in the power adapter, and press the power button.
  2. Connect to your Wi-Fi when asked. This part is important: the Mac needs internet to finish setting up.
  3. When you see a screen that says something like “Remote Management” or “MomsRising will automatically configure your Mac”, that’s expected. Continue through it.
  4. Create a password for the laptop itself when prompted, and write it down somewhere safe. (This is the password to unlock the computer, separate from your email password.)
  5. Finish the setup screens until you reach the desktop.
⏳Then just wait a bit. Over the next several minutes your apps (Google Chrome, Slack, 1Password, and Zoom) will install themselves automatically. You’ll see their icons appear in the dock at the bottom of the screen. Keep the Mac on and connected to Wi-Fi while this happens.
✅You don’t need to download anything yourself. If an app isn’t there yet, give it a little longer. They arrive over the internet.
2

Sign in to Google, your home base

Your MomsRising Google account unlocks everything else.

About 5 minutes

Your email lives at @momsrising.org, powered by Google Workspace (Gmail, Calendar, Drive, and Docs). Setting this up first matters, because you’ll use it to sign in to all your other tools.

  1. Find the welcome email from us with your new @momsrising.org address and temporary password.
  2. Open Google Chrome and go to accounts.google.com.
  3. Sign in with your new email and the temporary password.
  4. Create your own new password when prompted. Make it strong. You’ll save it in 1Password in Step 4.
  5. Add a phone number for account recovery if Google asks. This protects your account.
  6. Make Chrome your default browser. Chrome will usually pop up a banner asking. Click “Set as default.” (Or go to the Apple menu → System Settings → Desktop & Dock, scroll down to Default web browser, and choose Google Chrome.)
  7. Open Gmail and confirm you can see your inbox. 🎉
🛡️If Google asks you to turn on 2-step verification right now, skip it for the moment. We’ll set it up properly in Step 6 using 1Password. It’s easier once 1Password is ready.
🌐Setting Chrome as your default means links you click, including in Slack, open in Chrome where you’re already signed in, instead of Safari.
👀What you’ll see next: Your Slack and 1Password invites (Steps 3 and 4) will arrive in this inbox. So keep this tab open.
3

Join Slack, where the team talks

Sign in with your Google account in one click.

About 3 minutes
  1. In your @momsrising.org Gmail, find the Slack invitation email and click “Join Now.”
  2. When asked how to sign in, choose “Continue with Google” and pick your MomsRising account. (No new password needed!)
  3. Open the Slack app (it installed itself in Step 1) and sign in there too, so you get messages on your desktop.
  4. Add a profile photo and your name so the team knows who you are.
  5. Say a quick hello in your team’s welcome channel. 👋
💡Slack is where day-to-day conversation happens. Think of it like a friendly group text for work. It’s the fastest way to reach someone.
4

Set up 1Password, your password keeper

One safe place for all your work logins.

About 5 minutes
  1. In your @momsrising.org Gmail, find the 1Password invitation and click to accept it.
  2. Create your account password (this one unlocks 1Password itself, so make it memorable but strong).
  3. Save your Emergency Kit / Secret Key. 1Password will show you a one-time setup code. Download or print it and keep it somewhere safe. You cannot get back in without it.
  4. Install the 1Password extension for Chrome when prompted, so it can fill in passwords for you.
  5. Save your new Google/email password into 1Password so you never have to remember it again.
🔐From now on, let 1Password remember and fill in your work passwords. It’s safer than reusing the same password, and a lot less to memorize.
5

Sign in to Zoom for video calls

Sign in with Google. That’s the only step.

About 2 minutes
  1. Open the Zoom app (it installed itself in Step 1).
  2. On the sign-in screen, choose “Sign in with Google”, not email/password.
  3. Pick your @momsrising.org account. You’re in!
  4. Click your photo → Settings and do a quick test of your microphone and camera so you’re ready for your first meeting.
📹Important: Always use “Sign in with Google” for Zoom. If you make a separate Zoom password by accident, you may not land in the MomsRising account.
6

Add extra security, with 1Password doing the work

Turn on 2-step verification and let 1Password fill the codes for you.

About 5 minutes

2-step verification means signing in takes your password plus a short code that changes every 30 seconds. So even if someone learned your password, they still couldn’t get in. We’ll store that code generator right inside 1Password, so it fills in automatically. No separate phone app needed.

  1. In Chrome (signed in to your @momsrising.org account), go to myaccount.google.com/security.
  2. Click 2-Step Verification and follow the prompts to turn it on. (Google may ask you to confirm with the recovery phone number from Step 2, which is fine.)
  3. When you reach the options, choose “Authenticator” (sometimes shown as “Set up authenticator” or “Authenticator app”). Google will display a QR code on screen.
  4. Open 1Password, find your MomsRising email login, and click Edit. Add a field and choose “One-Time Password.”
  5. Click the little QR-code scan icon in 1Password and scan the QR code on your screen. 1Password starts generating a 6-digit code.
  6. Copy that 6-digit code from 1Password, paste it into Google to confirm, and Save.
  7. That’s it. Next time you sign in, 1Password fills the verification code for you automatically. ✅
💡Why this way? Keeping the code in 1Password means if you ever get a new phone, your sign-in still works, and you never have to dig out a separate app to find a code.
